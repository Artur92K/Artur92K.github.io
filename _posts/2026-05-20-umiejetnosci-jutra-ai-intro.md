## What Is "Umiejętności Jutra AI"?

**Umiejętności Jutra AI** (literally: *Skills of Tomorrow AI*) is a free, structured AI training program run by Google in Poland. It's delivered online, self-paced within a weekly schedule, and built around one central idea: AI as an active work partner, not a one-shot tool for generating text.

The program runs across **3 weeks** and uses **Gemini** as the primary demonstration tool - but this is deliberate framing, not lock-in. The course explicitly states that every principle taught applies equally to Claude, ChatGPT, and Copilot. You're learning how to think with AI, not how to use a specific interface.

For Polish speakers, registration is at [rsvp.withgoogle.com/events/umiejetnoscijutra](https://rsvp.withgoogle.com/events/umiejetnoscijutra).

## Why This Course Is Different

Most AI content online falls into two traps: either it's too shallow ("just ask ChatGPT!") or too technical for anyone without a CS background. This program threads that needle well.

**It's practical from lesson one.** The very first demo shows you taking a long PDF report, throwing it into an AI, and walking out with a polished presentation in minutes. No theory preamble, no "but first let me explain transformers." You see value in the first 3 minutes.

**It teaches the mental model shift explicitly.** The core framing is the shift from treating AI like a search engine (question → answer → done) to treating it like a colleague: you give context, source material, and expected output format - AI does the heavy lifting and delivers something ready to edit or use. That shift alone is worth the enrollment.

**It introduces the 3W Compass.** This framework from the program's inauguration gives you three questions to ask about any task in your workday that help you spot where AI can actually help - and then act on it immediately instead of filing it under "things I should try with AI someday."

**The principles are tool-agnostic by design.** Every demonstration comes with an implicit "and here's how this works in the other tools." You're not building Gemini muscle memory; you're building AI-fluency.

## Week 1 at a Glance

| Module | Topic | Type |
|--------|-------|------|
| 1 | Introduction to Generative AI | Optional - skip if you know the basics |
| 2 | Effective prompting fundamentals | Core - do not skip |
| 3 | Deep Research in minutes | Practical tools |
| 4 | Generating presentations and visuals | Practical tools |
| 5 | Building your first AI assistant | Week project |
| 6 | Using AI tools safely | Essential before you go further |

The optional flag on Module 1 is real - the course means it. If you already know what an LLM is and roughly how prompting works, jump straight to Module 2. There's no linear obligation.

## What You Actually Walk Away With

The goal of Week 1 is not knowledge - it's two concrete outputs:

1. **Your own AI assistant**, configured for a specific repeatable task in your work. Not a generic chatbot, but a tuned assistant that knows your context, your format preferences, and your expected output.

2. **A mapped list of tasks** in your daily work where AI can save you time *right now*. Not hypothetically, not after you learn more - now. The course calls this applying the 3W Compass in practice.

This is a harder deliverable than most courses attempt. Most courses end with "now you know the theory." This one ends with something you'll actually open tomorrow morning.
